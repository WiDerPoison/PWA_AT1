Studying is hard, boring and time consuming, yet essential. Below are some general tips on how to make your study efficent and reasonable. View the videos for further information.

  • Set Clear Goals: Break down your tasks into smaller, manageable goals to avoid feeling overwhelmed.
  • Create a Study Schedule: Plan your study time ahead. Use a calendar or planner to stay organized.
  • Prioritize Tasks: Tackle the most important or difficult tasks first when your energy is highest.
  • Minimize Distractions: Find a quiet place to study and turn off your phone notifications to stay focused.
  • Take Breaks: Study in short intervals (e.g., 25 minutes of studying, followed by a 5-minute break).
  • Use Active Learning: Engage with the material by teaching someone else, practicing problems, or summarizing what you’ve learned.
  • Stay Consistent: Regular, consistent study habits are more effective than cramming the night before.
  • Stay Hydrated and Eat Well: Fuel your brain with healthy snacks and plenty of water during study sessions.
  • Get Enough Sleep: Ensure you’re getting 7-9 hours of sleep to improve memory retention and focus.
  • Review Regularly: Go over your notes frequently to reinforce what you’ve learned.
